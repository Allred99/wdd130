Hit Dice

Hit Dice: 1d8 per monk level

Hit Points at 1st Level: 8 + your Constitution modifier

Hit Points at Higher Levels: 1d8 (or 5) + your Constitution modifier per monk level after 1st

Proficiencies

Armor: None

Weapons: Simple weapons, shortswords

Tools: Choose one type of artisan's tools or one musical instrument

Saving Throws: Strength, Dexterity

Skills: Choose 3 from Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Perception, Religion, Stealth

Equipment

You start with the following equipment in addition to the equipment granted by your background:

  • (a) a shortswor or (b) any simple weapon

  • (a) a dungeoneer's pack (b) an explorer's pack

1st Level: Martial Arts

Your practice in martial arts gives you mastery of combat styles that use unarmed strikes and monk weapons (which are all simple weapons and martial weapons without the Heavy or Two-Handed property)/

You gain the following benefits while you are unarmed or wielding only monk weapons, provided that you’re not wearing any armor or wielding a shield:

  • You become proficient with improvised weapons and can use your Martial Arts die for damage rolls you make with improvised weapons. Improvised weapons are considered to be monk weapons for you.

  • You can use Dexterity instead of Strength for the attack and damage rolls of your unarmed strikes and monk weapons.

  • You can roll a d6 in place of the normal damage of your unarmed strikes. This die changes as you gain monk levels, as shown in the Martial Arts column of the monk table.

  • Whenever you make an Athletics check, you can choose to use your Dexterity modifier instead of Strength.

  • You can use your bonus action to make a single unarmed strike.

  • If you hit a creature with an unarmed strike, you can use your bonus action to attempt to Grapple them.

1st Level: Unarmored Defense

While you’re not wearing any armor or wielding a shield, your base AC is equal to 10 + your Dexterity and Wisdom modifier.

1st Level: Weapon Specialty

You gain the Weapon Specialty: Monk Weapons feat and become proficient in all weapons in the Monk group if you aren’t already. All weapons in the Monk group are considered to be monk weapons for you.

2nd Level: Flurry of Blows

Once per round, you can use your bonus action to make two unarmed strikes. You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Monk level. Whenever you finish a short rest, you regain expended uses of this feature equal to your Wisdom modififer (minimum 1).

2nd Level: Unarmored Movement

While not wearing armor or wielding a shield, you gain a bonus to your movement speed as shown in the Unarmored Movement column of the monk table.

Additionally, while not wearing armor or wielding a shield, your movement does not trigger attacks of opportunity and you can take the dash or dodge action as a bonus action.

3rd Level: Heightened Reaction

Your training has enabled you to react quickly, granting you the following benefits:

  • Deflect Attack. You can use your reaction to deflect or catch a strike when you’re hit with an attack that would deal bludgeoning, piercing, or slashing damage. When you do, roll your martial arts die- reduce the damage you would have taken by that amount. If you reduce the bludgeoning, slashing or piercing damage to 0, you can make one unarmed strike against the creature if it’s in range as part of the same reaction. If the attack was a ranged attack, and you reduced the damage to 0, you can throw the missile back if the missile is small enough to hold in one hand and you have at least one free hand. The attack made this way uses your proficiency bonus and Dexterity modifier for the attack and damage roll, and its range becomes 60 ft.

  • Emboldened Initiative. Whenever you roll initiative, you can add your Wisdom modifier to the roll.

3rd Level: Monk Subclass

You gain one monk subclass of your choice. You gain each of your subclass’s features that are of your monk level and lower. This class’s description tells you the levels when your subclass provides features.

4th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify

4th Level: Slow Fall

Any fall damage that you would take is reduced by an amount equal to five times your monk level.

5th Level: Extra Attack

You can attack twice, instead of once, whenever you take the attack action on your turn.

5th Level: Stunning Strike

Once per turn, when you hit another creature with a melee weapon attack or unarmed strike, you can attempt to give the creature the Stunned condition. The creature must make a Constitution saving throw. The DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1). On a failed saving throw, the creature is Stunned until the end of your next turn. On a successful saving throw, the creature is instead Dazed (can use their movement or action, not both and cannot take bonus actions or reactions) until the end of your next turn. You cannot use this feature on a creature that is already Stunned or Dazed from this feature.

You can use this feature a number of times per short or long rest equal to your monk level.

6th Level: Accelerated Reaction

You now have two reactions per round, instead of one.

6th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your monk subclass.

7th Level: Evasion

When you are subjected to an effect that allows you to make a Dexterity saving throw to only take half damage, you instead take no damage if you succeed and only half damage if you fail.

7th Level: Heightened Metabolism

Once per long rest, if you spend at least one minute resting, you can gain the benefits of a short rest.

8th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

8th Level: Spiritual Guidance

You learn the Guidance cantrip. Additionally, you can cast Enhance Ability at its base level as an action without using a spell slot or material components a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1).

9th Level: Acrobatic Movement

While not wearing armor or wielding a shield, you gain the ability to move along vertical surfaces and across liquids on your turn without falling during the movement.

9th Level: Purity of Body and Mind

You cannot be Charmed, Frightened, Paralyzed, Poisoned, or Stunned and you become immune to Poison and Psychic damage. Additionally, forgoing food or drink doesn’t give you levels of exhaustion.

10th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

10th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your monk subclass.

11th Level: Deflect Energy

You can use the Deflect Attack option of your Heightened Reaction feature against ranged attacks that deal any type of damage.

12th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

13th Level: Disciplined Soul

You become proficient in all saving throws. Additionally, a number of times per short or long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1), whenever you make a saving throw and fail, you can choose to reroll it, but you must take the new result.

14th Level: Subclass Feature

You gain a feature from your monk subclass.

16th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

18th Level: Superior Defense

As a bonus action, you can become resistant to all damage types except for force damage for the next minute. If you are Incapacitated within the minute, this effect ends early. You can use this feature twice per long rest.

19th Level: Feat

You gain the Ability Score Improvement feat or another feat of your choice for which you qualify.

20th Level: Enlightenment

You gain the following benefits:

  • Your Dexterity and Wisdom scores increase by 2 and their maximums are now 22.

  • You gain 30 ft. of truesight.

  • Once per long rest, you can cast Foresight without expending a spell slot or needing material components. However, the duration of the spell becomes 4 hours.

Way of the Auric

Through your training, you’ve learned to harness aura- a form of life energy that radiates around and within the living. Those that can see the aura of others can discern their emotions based on the color of their aura.

3rd Level: Auric Vision

You can see the colors of the auras of living creatures within 30 ft. of you- even if they’re hidden or Invisible. The color of their aura varies based on their mood. You have advantage on Insight checks against a creature if you can see the color of their aura. As long as you can see a creature’s aura, they cannot gain any benefits from being Invisible to you. This feature does not work on constructs or the undead.

3rd Level: Mood Boost

As a bonus action, you can channel your emotions to empower yourself or a creature within 15 ft. of you. The effect that you give depends on the color of aura you choose to radiate.

  • Red. Their damage rolls are increased by 2 until the start of your next turn.

  • Orange. They have advantage on saving throws against being frightened until the start of your next turn.

  • Yellow. Their movement speed increases by 10 ft. until the start of your next turn.

  • Green. They recover 2d4 hit points.

  • Blue. They gain a d6 inspiration die that lasts for the next minute.

  • Indigo. They have advantage on saving throws against being Charmed until the start of your next turn.

  • Violet. Their Intelligence and Charisma checks are increased by 2 until the start of your next turn.

  • Magenta. All damage the creature takes is reduced by your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1) until the start of your next turn.

  • White. All of their rolls are increased by 1 until the start of your next turn.

  • Black. Their attack rolls are increased by 2 until the start of your next turn.

You can use this feature a number of times per long rest equal to your Wisdom modifier (minimum 1). You regain one expended use of this feature whenever you finish a short rest.

6th Level: Auric Blast

You learn how to manifest your aura to blast your foes. As an action, you can make two ranged spell attacks- each with a range of 60 ft. You are proficient with it and you add your Wisdom modifier to its attack and damage rolls. On a hit, a creature suffers force damage equal to two rolls of your Martial Arts die.

10th Level: Empowered Mood

The range of your Mood Boost feature doubles to 30 ft. and the benefits of your Mood Boost feature double. For example, a creature gains a d12 inspiration die if you choose blue and a creature’s damage is reduced by double your Wisdom modifier if you choose magenta. If you choose orange or indigo, and the creature is currently Frightened (orange) or Charmed (indigo), you can end the condition.

10th Level: Surging Aura

Whenever you roll initiative, you gain a number of temporary hit points equal to your monk level. These temporary hit points last 1 minute.

14th Level: Forced Empathy

You learn to alter a creature’s life energy to mimic the injuries of others. Once per short or long rest, as a reaction, when a creature within 30 ft. of you takes damage, you can use your reaction to choose another creature within 30 ft. of you feel the same pain that they did. The chosen creature must make a Constitution saving throw. The DC is 8 + your proficiency bonus + your Wisdom modifier. If the creature fails, they lose the same amount of hit points as the other creature did. On a successful saving throw, the creature only loses half the hit points that the other creature lost.
